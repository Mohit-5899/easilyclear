This section covers the use of inland and ocean waterways for transport, as well as the role of pipelines in moving goods.

## Waterways

Waterways are an important mode of transport. India utilizes both inland waterways and ocean routes for trade. Approximately 95 per cent of the country's trade volume (68 per cent in terms of value) is moved by sea.

### Inland Waterways
India has several significant inland waterways, including:
- **National Waterway No. 1:** The Ganga river between Prayagraj and Haldia (1620 km).
- **National Waterway No. 2:** The Brahmaputra river between Sadiya and Dhubri (891 km).
- **National Waterway No. 3:** The West-Coast Canal in Kerala (Kottapurma-Kollam, Udyogamandal, and Champakkara canals—205 km).
- **National Waterway No. 4:** Specified stretches of the Godavari and Krishna rivers, along with the Kakinada-Puducherry stretch of canals (1078 km).
- **National Waterway No. 5:** Specified stretches of the river Brahmani, along with the Matai river, delta channels of the Mahanadi and Brahmani rivers, and the East Coast Canal (588 km).

Other substantial inland waterways include the Mandavi, Zuari, and Cumberjua, Sunderbans, Barak, and the backwaters of Kerala.

### Ocean Ports
With a coastline of 11,098.81 km, India has 12 major ports and 200 notified non-major (minor/intermediate) ports. Major ports handle 95 per cent of India's foreign trade.

**Key Ports and their Characteristics:**
- **Deendayal Port (Kandla):** A tidal port in Kuchchh; developed after Independence to ease trade volume from the Mumbai port following the loss of Karachi port to Pakistan. It serves the industrial and granary belts of Jammu and Kashmir, Himachal Pradesh, Punjab, Haryana, Rajasthan, and Gujarat.
- **Mumbai:** The largest port with a spacious natural and well-sheltered harbour.
- **Jawaharlal Nehru Port:** Planned to decongest the Mumbai port and serve as a regional hub.
- **Mormugao (Goa):** The premier iron ore exporting port, accounting for about 50 per cent of India's iron ore exports.
- **New Mangalore:** Caters to the export of iron ore concentrates from Kudremukh mines.
- **Cochin:** Located at the entrance of a lagoon with a natural harbour; it is the extreme south-western port.
- **V.O. Chidambaranar (Tuticorin):** Located in Tamil Nadu, this extreme south-eastern port has a natural harbour and rich hinterland.
- **Chennai:** One of the oldest artificial ports; it ranks second to Mumbai in terms of trade volume and cargo.
- **Vishakhapatnam:** The deepest landlocked and well-protected port, originally conceived as an outlet for iron ore exports.
- **Paradwip (Odisha):** Specializes in the export of iron ore.
- **Shyama Prasad Mookerjee (Kolkata):** An inland riverine port serving the Ganga-Brahmaputra basin. As a tidal port, it requires constant dredging of the Hoogly.
- **Haldia:** Developed as a subsidiary port to relieve pressure on the Kolkata port.

## Pipelines

Pipelines are an emerging mode of transport used to move goods. One of the primary advantages of using pipelines is that they reduce trans-shipment losses and delays.
