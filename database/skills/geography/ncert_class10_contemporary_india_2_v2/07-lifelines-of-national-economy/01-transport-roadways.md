Roadways serve as a vital component of India's transport infrastructure, facilitating the movement of goods and services from supply locales to demand locations. India possesses the second largest road network in the world, totaling approximately 62.16 lakh km as of 2020–21.

## Importance of Road Transport

In India, roadways preceded the development of railways and continue to hold several advantages over rail transport:

- **Lower Construction Costs:** Building roads is significantly less expensive than laying railway lines.
- **Topographical Flexibility:** Roads can traverse dissected, undulating, and mountainous terrain, such as the Himalayas, by negotiating higher gradients of slopes.
- **Economic Efficiency for Small Loads:** Road transport is more economical when moving a small number of people or relatively small amounts of goods over short distances.
- **Door-to-Door Service:** Unlike other modes, roads provide direct service to specific locations, which reduces the costs associated with loading and unloading.
- **Feeder Role:** Roadways act as a link to other modes of transport, connecting railway stations, airports, and seaports.

## Classification of Roads

Indian roads are classified into six distinct categories based on their capacity and purpose:

- **Golden Quadrilateral Super Highways:** A major project implemented by the National Highway Authority of India (NHAI) to link mega cities (Delhi, Kolkata, Chennai, and Mumbai) via six-lane highways. This project also includes:
    - **North-South Corridor:** Linking Srinagar (Jammu & Kashmir) to Kanniyakumari (Tamil Nadu).
    - **East-West Corridor:** Connecting Silchar (Assam) to Porbander (Gujarat).
- **National Highways:** The primary road system that links the extreme parts of the country, running in both North-South and East-West directions.
- **State Highways:** These roads connect state capitals with various district headquarters.
- **District Roads:** These connect district headquarters to other locations within the same district.
- **Other Roads (Rural Roads):** These link rural areas and villages to towns. Under the *Pradhan Mantri Grameen Sadak Yojana*, provisions are made to ensure every village is linked to a major town by an all-season motorable road.
- **Border Roads:** Constructed and maintained by the Border Roads Organisation (established in 1960), these roads are of strategic importance in the northern and north-eastern border areas, improving accessibility in difficult terrain.

**Key facts:**
- **Total Road Network:** ~62.16 lakh km (2020–21).
- **Global Rank:** India has the second largest road network in the world.
- **Implementing Agency (Super Highways):** National Highway Authority of India (NHAI).
- **Strategic Agency:** Border Roads Organisation (established 1960).
- **Rural Development Scheme:** Pradhan Mantri Grameen Sadak Yojana.
