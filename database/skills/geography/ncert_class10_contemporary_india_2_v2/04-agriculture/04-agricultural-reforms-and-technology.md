This section examines the various institutional and technological reforms implemented to support the agricultural sector, as well as the social movements aimed at land redistribution.

## Institutional and Financial Reforms

To protect farmers from economic instability and exploitation, the Government of India has introduced several financial and protective measures:

- **Credit and Insurance:**
  - Establishment of Grameen banks, cooperative societies, and banks to provide loans at lower interest rates.
  - Provision for crop insurance to mitigate risks from drought, floods, cyclones, fire, and disease.
  - Introduction of the **Kisan Credit Card (KCC)** and the **Personal Accident Insurance Scheme (PAIS)**.
- **Price Support:**
  - The government announces **Minimum Support Prices (MSP)**, as well as remunerative and procurement prices for essential crops. These measures are designed to prevent exploitation by middlemen and speculators.
- **Information Dissemination:**
  - Special weather bulletins and agricultural programs are broadcast via radio and television to keep farmers informed.
  - Digital resources, such as the Farmers' Portal (farmer.gov.in), provide centralized information regarding agriculture, horticulture, and various government schemes.

## The Bhoodan–Gramdan Movement

Led by **Vinoba Bhave**, a follower of Mahatma Gandhi and a proponent of *gram swarajya*, the Bhoodan–Gramdan movement sought to address landlessness through voluntary redistribution.

- **Bhoodan (Land Gift):** The movement began in Pochampally, Telangana, when Shri Ram Chandra Reddy voluntarily offered 80 acres of land to be distributed among 80 landless villagers.
- **Gramdan (Village Gift):** Following the success of Bhoodan, some zamindars and village owners offered to distribute entire villages among the landless.
- **The "Bloodless Revolution":** Because these land transfers were achieved through voluntary donation rather than through violent conflict, the movement is often referred to as the "Bloodless Revolution." 

Note that some landowners participated in these movements partly due to the implementation of the land ceiling act.

**Key facts:**
- **Leader:** Vinoba Bhave (spiritual heir to Mahatma Gandhi).
- **Bhoodan Origin:** Pochampally, Telangana.
- **Bhoodan–Gramdan Alias:** The "Bloodless Revolution."
- **Key Financial Tools:** Kisan Credit Card (KCC) and Personal Accident Insurance Scheme (PAIS).
