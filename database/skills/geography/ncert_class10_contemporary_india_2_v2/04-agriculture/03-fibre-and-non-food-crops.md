Fibre crops are essential agricultural products used primarily for textiles and industrial applications. In India, the four major fibre crops are cotton, jute, hemp, and natural silk.

## Major Fibre Crops

### Cotton
India is considered the original home of the cotton plant and stands as the second-largest producer of cotton in the world, following China. It serves as a primary raw material for the cotton textile industry.

- **Soil and Climate Requirements:** Cotton grows best in the drier parts of the black cotton soil found in the Deccan plateau. Its growth requires:
    - High temperatures.
    - Light rainfall or irrigation.
    - 210 frost-free days.
    - Bright sunshine.
- **Growth Cycle:** It is a *kharif* crop that requires 6 to 8 months to reach maturity.

### Jute
Often referred to as the "golden fibre," jute is a vital industrial crop.

- **Soil and Climate Requirements:** Jute thrives in well-drained, fertile soils located in flood plains, where the soil is renewed annually. High temperatures are necessary during the growing period.
- **Major Producing States:** West Bengal, Bihar, Assam, Odisha, and Meghalaya.
- **Uses:** It is used to manufacture:
    - Gunny bags.
    - Mats and carpets.
    - Ropes and yarn.
    - Various other artefacts.

### Silk
Unlike cotton, jute, and hemp, which are derived from plants grown in the soil, silk is obtained from the cocoons of silkworms. These worms are fed on specific green leaves, most notably mulberry. The process of rearing silkworms for silk production is known as **sericulture**.

**Key facts:**
- **Major fibre crops in India:** Cotton, jute, hemp, and natural silk.
- **Cotton's global rank:** Second largest producer (after China).
- **Cotton's climate need:** 210 frost-free days.
- **Jute's nickname:** The golden fibre.
- **Sericulture:** The practice of rearing silkworms to produce silk.
