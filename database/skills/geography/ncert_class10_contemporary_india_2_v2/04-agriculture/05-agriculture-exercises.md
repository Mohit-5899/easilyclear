This section provides a variety of review exercises designed to test your understanding of agricultural systems, crop types, and government reforms.

## Review Questions

### Multiple Choice
Test your knowledge of specific agricultural definitions and classifications:
- Identify the system of agriculture where a single crop is grown over a large area.
- Distinguish between different cropping seasons, such as identifying a **rabi** crop.
- Recognize specific categories of crops, such as **leguminous** crops.

### Short Answer Questions (approx. 30 words)
Provide concise responses to the following:
- Identify an important beverage crop and the specific geographical conditions needed for its growth.
- Name a staple crop of India and the specific regions where it is cultivated.
- List the various institutional reform programmes introduced by the government to support farmers.

### Long Answer Questions (approx. 120 words)
Develop more detailed explanations for the following:
- Discuss the initiatives taken by the government to ensure an increase in agricultural production.
- Describe the specific geographical conditions required for the successful growth of rice.

## Activities and Practical Application

- **Map Work:** Use an outline map of India to locate and shade the primary wheat-producing areas.
- **Group Discussion:** Engage in a discussion regarding the necessity of literacy among farmers.
- **Word Search:** Complete a puzzle to find hidden terms related to Indian agriculture, including:
    - Staple food crops.
    - Summer cropping seasons.
    - Nutrients found in pulses like arhar, moong, gram, and urad.
    - Coarse grains.
    - Major beverage crops.
    - Fibers grown on black soils.

**Key facts:**
- **Cropping Seasons:** Includes the **kharif** (summer) season.
- **Crop Categories:** Includes **rabi** crops, **leguminous** crops (pulses), **staple** crops, **beverage** crops, and **coarse grains**.
- **Key Crops Mentioned:** Wheat, rice, coffee, cotton, and jowar.
