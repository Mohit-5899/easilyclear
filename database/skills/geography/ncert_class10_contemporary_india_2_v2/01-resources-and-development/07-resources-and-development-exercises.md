This section provides review exercises, discussion prompts, and activities designed to test understanding of resources, land use, and soil types.

## Review Questions

### Multiple Choice Questions
- **Land Degradation:** Identify the primary cause of land degradation in Punjab (e.g., intensive cultivation, deforestation, over-irrigation, or overgrazing).
- **Cultivation Methods:** Determine which state practices terrace cultivation (e.g., Punjab, Haryana, plains of Uttar Pradesh, or Uttarakhand).
- **Soil Distribution:** Identify which state contains predominant black soil (e.g., Uttar Pradesh, Maharashtra, Rajasthan, or Jharkhand).

### Short Answer Questions (approx. 30 words)
- Identify three states characterized by black soil and name the primary crop grown in that soil.
- Describe the soil found in the river deltas of the eastern coast and list three of its main features.
- Propose specific steps to control soil erosion in hilly regions.

### Long Answer Questions (approx. 120 words)
- Analyze the land use pattern in India and explain the reasons why forest cover has not significantly increased since 1960-61.
- Discuss the relationship between technical and economic development and the increased consumption of resources.

## Activities and Discussions

- **Local Project:** Create a project documenting the consumption and conservation of resources within your own locality.
- **School Resource Management:** Participate in a class discussion regarding methods to conserve various resources used in a school setting.
- **Scenario Analysis:** Reflect on the potential impact on modern lifestyles if global oil supplies were to become exhausted.
- **Word Puzzle:** Solve a search puzzle to identify key terms related to:
    - Natural endowments (land, water, vegetation, and minerals).
    - Non-renewable resources.
    - Soils with high water-retaining capacity.
    - Intensively leached soils of the monsoon climate.
    - Large-scale tree plantation used to check soil erosion.
    - The soil composition of the Great Plains of India.

**Key facts:**
- **Punjab land degradation cause:** Over-irrigation.
- **Terrace cultivation location:** Uttarakhand.
- **Black soil location:** Maharashtra.
