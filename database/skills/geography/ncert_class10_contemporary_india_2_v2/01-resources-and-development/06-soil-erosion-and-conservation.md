Soil erosion is the process of denudation of the soil cover and its subsequent washing away. While soil formation and erosion generally occur simultaneously in a state of balance, various factors can disturb this equilibrium, leading to the loss of fertile topsoil.

## Causes of Soil Erosion

Soil erosion is driven by both natural forces and human interventions:

- **Natural Forces:**
    - **Water:** Running water can cut through clayey soils to create deep channels called gullies. When water flows as a sheet over large areas down a slope, it results in sheet erosion.
    - **Wind:** Wind blows loose soil off flat or sloping land, a process known as wind erosion.
    - **Glaciers:** Glacial movement also contributes to the denudation of soil.
- **Human Activities:**
    - Deforestation
    - Over-grazing
    - Construction and mining
    - Defective farming methods, such as ploughing up and down a slope, which creates channels for rapid water flow.

When gully erosion makes land unfit for cultivation, it is referred to as "bad land." In the Chambal basin, these eroded areas are specifically called ravines.

## Soil Conservation Techniques

To mitigate the loss of soil, several agricultural and environmental techniques can be employed:

- **Contour Ploughing:** Ploughing along the contour lines of a slope rather than up and down it. This helps decelerate the flow of water down the slopes.
- **Terrace Cultivation:** Cutting steps into slopes to create terraces. This method restricts erosion and is widely used in the western and central Himalayas.
- **Strip Cropping:** Dividing large fields into strips and leaving strips of grass to grow between crops. This practice breaks the force of the wind.
- **Shelter Belts:** Planting lines of trees to create shelter. These rows of trees help stabilize the soil and have been significant in stabilizing sand dunes and deserts in western India.

**Key facts:**
- **Gully erosion:** Creates deep channels; in the Chambal basin, these are called **ravines**.
- **Sheet erosion:** Occurs when water flows as a sheet over large areas of a slope.
- **Wind erosion:** The removal of loose soil by wind on flat or sloping land.
- **Contour ploughing:** A method used to slow down water flow on slopes.
- **Shelter belts:** Rows of trees used to stabilize sand dunes and desert regions.
