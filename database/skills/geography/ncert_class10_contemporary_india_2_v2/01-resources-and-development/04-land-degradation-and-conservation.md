Land degradation refers to the decline in the quality and productivity of land, driven by both natural forces and human activities. Since 95% of basic human needs—including food, shelter, and clothing—are derived from land, managing its degradation is critical for both current and future generations.

## Causes of Land Degradation

Human activities have significantly accelerated the pace of natural land damage. The specific causes often vary by region and industry:

- **Deforestation and Mining:** Mining and quarrying leave deep scars and traces of overburdening once excavation is complete. In states such as Jharkhand, Chhattisgarh, Madhya Pradesh, and Odisha, deforestation caused by mining has led to severe degradation.
- **Overgrazing:** This is a primary driver of land degradation in states including Gujarat, Rajasthan, Madhya Pradesh, and Maharashtra.
- **Over-irrigation:** In Punjab, Haryana, and western Uttar Pradesh, excessive irrigation leads to waterlogging, which increases the salinity and alkalinity of the soil.
- **Mineral Processing:** The grinding of materials like limestone (for the cement industry) or calcite and soapstone (for the ceramic industry) generates massive amounts of atmospheric dust. When this dust settles, it retards the infiltration of water into the soil.
- **Industrial Effluents:** In recent years, the discharge of industrial waste has become a major source of both land and water pollution in many parts of the country.

## Conservation Measures

Various strategies can be implemented to mitigate land degradation depending on the environment:

- **Arid Area Management:**
    - Afforestation and planting shelter belts of plants.
    - Controlling overgrazing.
    - Stabilizing sand dunes by growing thorny bushes.
- **Industrial and Suburban Management:**
    - Proper management of waste lands.
    - Controlling mining activities.
    - Ensuring the proper discharge and disposal of industrial effluents and wastes only after they have been treated.

**Key facts:**
- **95%** of basic human needs are obtained from land.
- **Mining-induced deforestation** is most severe in Jharkhand, Chhattisgarh, Madhya Pradesh, and Odisha.
- **Overgrazing** is a major issue in Gujarat, Rajasthan, Madhya Pradesh, and Maharashtra.
- **Waterlogging, salinity, and alkalinity** are caused by over-irrigation in Punjab, Haryana, and western Uttar Pradesh.
