This topic examines the physical relief features of India and the various ways land is utilized across the country. As a finite natural resource, land supports vegetation, wildlife, human life, economic activities, and transport systems.

## Relief Features of India

India's land is distributed across several distinct relief features, each providing different ecological and economic advantages:

- **Plains (43%):** The largest portion of India's land, providing essential facilities for agriculture and industry.
- **Mountains (30%):** These areas ensure the perennial flow of several rivers, support tourism, and provide significant ecological benefits.
- **Plateaus (27%):** This region is characterized by rich reserves of minerals, fossil fuels, and forests.
- **Islands:** Additional landmasses that contribute to the country's total geography.

## Land Use Patterns

The pattern of land use is determined by a combination of physical factors (topography, climate, and soil types) and human factors (population density, technological capability, culture, and traditions).

Land resources are categorized into several specific uses:

- **Forests:** Essential for ecological balance, though current forest area is lower than the 33% target outlined in the National Forest Policy (1952).
- **Land not available for cultivation:**
    - Barren and waste land (including rocky, arid, and desert areas).
    - Land put to non-agricultural uses (e.g., buildings, roads, railways, factories, and settlements).
- **Other uncultivated land:**
    - Permanent pastures and grazing land.
    - Land under miscellaneous tree crops/groves (not included in net sown area).
    - Culturable waste land (left uncultivated for more than five agricultural years).
- **Fallow lands:**
    - **Current fallow:** Left without cultivation for one or less than one agricultural year.
    - **Other than current fallow:** Left uncultivated for the past 1 to 5 agricultural years.
- **Net Sown Area (NSA):** The physical extent of land on which crops are sown and harvested.
- **Gross Cropped Area:** The sum of the net sown area plus the area sown more than once in an agricultural year.

## Data and Reporting

The total geographical area of India is 3.28 million sq km. However, land use data is only available for 93% of this area. This is due to incomplete reporting in most North-Eastern states (except Assam) and unsurveyed areas in Jammu and Kashmir occupied by Pakistan and China.

**Key facts:**
- **Total Area:** 3.28 million sq km.
- **Relief Distribution:** Plains (~43%), Mountains (~30%), Plateaus (~27%).
- **National Forest Policy (1952) Target:** 33% of geographical area should be under forest.
- **NSA Variation:** Over 80% in Punjab and Haryana; less than 10% in Arunachal Pradesh, Mizoram, Manipur, and Andaman Nicobar Islands.
