Biodiversity, or biological diversity, refers to the immense richness of life on Earth, encompassing a vast array of wildlife and cultivated species. These organisms vary significantly in form and function but are closely integrated into a complex ecological system through multiple networks of interdependencies.

## The Ecological Web

Humans are not separate from nature but are part of a complex web of ecological systems. Our existence is deeply dependent on the services provided by other living organisms, ranging from microscopic bacteria and lichens to massive organisms like banyan trees, elephants, and blue whales.

The interdependence within this system is vital for human survival:
- **Air and Water:** Plants, animals, and micro-organisms work together to recreate the quality of the air we breathe and the water we drink.
- **Soil and Food:** These organisms maintain the quality of the soil required to produce our food.
- **Primary Production:** Forests serve a critical role in the ecological system, acting as primary producers upon which all other living beings depend.

## Biodiversity in India

India is recognized as one of the world’s richest countries regarding biological diversity. The country possesses a vast array of species, and it is estimated that the current known variety may be only a fraction of the total, with the actual number potentially being twice or thrice what has been discovered.

While these diverse flora and fauna are deeply integrated into daily human life, they are currently under significant stress. This pressure is largely driven by human insensitivity toward the environment.

**Key facts:**
- **Definition:** Biodiversity is the richness of species (wildlife and cultivated) integrated through networks of interdependency.
- **India's Status:** One of the world's richest countries in terms of biological diversity.
- **Ecological Role of Forests:** They function as primary producers for the entire ecological system.
- **Current Threat:** Biodiversity is under great stress due to environmental insensitivity.
