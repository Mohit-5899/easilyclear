This section provides review questions and activities designed to test your understanding of forest and wildlife conservation, community involvement, and biodiversity.

## Multiple Choice and Matching

Test your ability to distinguish between different conservation strategies and forest classifications:

- **Conservation Strategies:** Identify which methods involve direct community participation (such as the Chipko Movement or Beej Bachao Andolan) versus those that are purely administrative (such as the demarcation of wildlife sanctuaries).
- **Forest Classifications:** Match the following definitions to their correct terms:
    - **Reserved forests:** Regarded as the most valuable for the conservation of forest and wildlife resources.
    - **Protected forests:** Forest lands that are protected from any further depletion.
    - **Unclassed forests:** Other forests and wastelands belonging to both government and private individuals and communities.

## Short Answer Questions

Prepare for concise responses (approximately 30 words) on the following fundamental concepts:

- **Biodiversity:** Define biodiversity and explain its significance to human life.
- **Human Impact:** Explain how human activities have contributed to the depletion of flora and fauna.

## Long Answer Questions

Develop detailed responses (approximately 120 words) to demonstrate a deeper understanding of conservation dynamics:

- **Community Conservation:** Describe the specific ways in which various communities in India have worked to conserve and protect forests and wildlife.
- **Conservation Practices:** Write a detailed note regarding good practices and effective methods used toward the conservation of forest and wildlife resources.

**Key facts:**
- **Reserved forests** are the most valuable for conservation.
- **Protected forests** are managed to prevent further depletion.
- **Unclassed forests** include both government and private/community-owned lands.
