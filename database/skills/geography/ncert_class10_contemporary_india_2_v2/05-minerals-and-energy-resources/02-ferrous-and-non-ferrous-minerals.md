Metallic minerals in India are categorized into ferrous and non-ferrous minerals. Ferrous minerals, which contain iron, account for approximately three-fourths of the total value of metallic mineral production and serve as the foundation for metallurgical industries.

## Ferrous Minerals

### Iron Ore
Iron ore is the backbone of industrial development. India possesses abundant, high-quality reserves, primarily categorized into two types:
- **Magnetite:** The finest iron ore with an iron content of up to 70%. It possesses excellent magnetic qualities, making it highly valuable for the electrical industry.
- **Hematite:** The most important industrial iron ore due to the large quantities used, though it has a slightly lower iron content (50–60%).

**Major Iron Ore Belts:**
- **Odisha-Jharkhand Belt:** High-grade hematite is found in the Badampahar mines (Mayurbhanj and Kendujhar districts of Odisha) and the Singbhum district of Jharkhand (Gua and Noamundi mines).
- **Durg-Bastar-Chandrapur Belt:** Located in Chhattisgarh and Maharashtra. The Bailadila range in Chhattisgarh's Bastar district contains 14 deposits of super high-grade hematite, which is exported to Japan and South Korea via Vishakhapatnam port.
- **Ballari-Chitradurga-Chikkamagaluru-Tumakuru Belt:** Located in Karnataka. The Kudremukh mines in the Western Ghats are a 100% export unit and are among the largest in the world; ore is transported as slurry via pipeline to Mangaluru.
- **Maharashtra-Goa Belt:** Includes Goa and the Ratnagiri district of Maharashtra. While the ores are not of very high quality, they are efficiently exploited and exported through Marmagao port.

### Manganese
Manganese is essential for manufacturing steel and ferro-manganese alloys. Approximately 10 kg of manganese is required to produce one tonne of steel. It is also utilized in the production of paints, insecticides, and bleaching powder.

## Non-Ferrous Minerals
While India's reserves and production of non-ferrous minerals are not considered highly satisfactory, they are vital to the engineering, electrical, and metallurgical industries.

### Copper
India faces a critical deficiency in copper reserves and production. Due to its ductility, malleability, and high conductivity, copper is primarily used in chemical, electronic, and electrical cable industries. Leading producers include:
- Balaghat mines (Madhya Pradesh)
- Khetri mines (Rajasthan)
- Singhbhum district (Jharkhand)

### Bauxite
Aluminium is obtained from bauxite, a clay-like substance formed by the decomposition of rocks rich in aluminium silicates. Aluminium is valued for combining the strength of metals like iron with extreme lightness, malleability, and good conductivity.
- **Major Deposits:** Found in the Maikal hills, the Amarkantak plateau, and the Bilaspur-Katni plateau region.
- **Leading Producer:** Odisha was the largest bauxite producer in 2018–19, with the Panchpatmali deposits in the Koraput district being the most significant.

**Key facts:**
- **Iron Ore Production (2018–19):** 97% came from Odisha, Chhattisgarh, Karnataka, and Jharkhand.
- **Bauxite Production (2018–19):** Odisha (65%) and Jharkhand (10%) were major contributors.
- **Steel Requirement:** 10 kg of manganese per tonne of steel.
- **Magnetite Iron Content:** Up to 70%.
