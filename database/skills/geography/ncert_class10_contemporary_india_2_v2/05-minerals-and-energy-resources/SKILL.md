Minerals are indispensable substances that form the foundation of modern life, ranging from the tiny pins we use daily to the massive structures of ships and skyscrapers. They are essential for industrial development, transportation, and even biological life processes.

## Understanding Minerals

Geologists define a mineral as a "homogenous, naturally occurring substance with a definable internal structure." While rocks are combinations of these substances, a single rock may consist of one mineral (like limestone) or several minerals in varying proportions.

The study of minerals is approached differently depending on the discipline:
- **Geographers** study the distribution of mineral resources and their associated economic activities to understand landforms.
- **Geologists** focus on the formation, age, and physical and chemical composition of minerals.

## Occurrence and Extraction

Minerals are typically found in **ores**, which are accumulations of minerals mixed with other elements. For an ore to be commercially viable, the mineral content must be in sufficient concentration. The mode of occurrence determines the ease and cost of extraction:

- **Igneous and Metamorphic Rocks:** Minerals occur in cracks, crevices, faults, or joints. Smaller occurrences are called **veins**, while larger ones are called **lodes**. Common examples include tin, copper, zinc, and lead.
- **Sedimentary Rocks:** Minerals are deposited in horizontal layers or beds. This includes coal and some iron ores (formed under heat and pressure) as well as gypsum, potash salt, and sodium salt (formed through evaporation).
- **Decomposition:** Surface rocks may decompose, leaving behind a residual mass of weathered material containing ores, such as bauxite.
- **Placer Deposits:** Found in alluvial sands of valley floors or hill bases, these consist of minerals that are not corroded by water, such as gold, silver, tin, and platinum.
- **Ocean Waters:** While many minerals are too widely diffused to be economic, ocean waters provide common salt, magnesium, and bromine, while ocean beds contain manganese nodules.

**Key facts:**
- **Definition:** A mineral is a homogenous, naturally occurring substance with a definable internal structure.
- **Veins vs. Lodes:** Veins are smaller mineral occurrences in igneous/metamorphic rocks; lodes are larger.
- **Economic Viability:** Depends on mineral concentration in the ore, ease of extraction, and proximity to markets.
