This section provides review questions and activities designed to test your understanding of manufacturing industries, their classifications, and their environmental impacts.

## Review Activities

Use the following prompts to test your knowledge of the manufacturing sector:

### Classification Exercises
- **Weight and Bulk:** Classify the following items into two groups: **Heavy Industries** or **Light Industries**:
    - Oil
    - Knitting needles
    - Brassware
    - Fuse wires
    - Watches
    - Sewing Machines
    - Shipbuilding
    - Electric Bulbs
    - Paint brushes
    - Automobiles
- **Raw Materials:** Identify whether the following industries are **Agro-based** or **Mineral-based**:
    - Cotton textile
    - Iron and steel
    - Sugar
    - Aluminium smelting
    - Jute
    - Petrochemicals

### Critical Thinking Questions
- **Textiles:** Why did Mahatma Gandhi place such heavy emphasis on spinning yarn and weaving *khadi*?
- **Industry Structure:** Why is it important for India to maintain a higher proportion of power looms and handlooms compared to the mill sector?
- **Sugar Industry:** The sugar industry is seasonal in nature. Explain why this makes it ideally suited for the cooperative sector.
- **Environmental Impact:** Thermal pollution occurs when hot water from factories is drained into rivers. What are the potential effects of this on aquatic life?
- **Resource Management:** Given that every litre of waste water discharged by industry can pollute eight times that quantity of freshwater, suggest methods to reduce industrial water pollution.

## Key facts:
- **Manufacturing Definition:** The process of converting raw materials into more valuable finished products.
- **Economic Indicator:** The economic strength of a country is often measured by the development of its manufacturing industries.
- **Textile Importance:** The textile industry is the only industry in India that is self-reliant and complete in the value chain, from raw material to finished product.
- **Iron and Steel Ratio:** To produce steel, iron ore, coking coal, and limestone are required in a ratio of approximately **4 : 2 : 1**.
- **Pollution Types:** Industrial activities contribute to four main types of pollution: **Air, Water, Land, and Noise**.
