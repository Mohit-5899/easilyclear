Non-metallic minerals include essential substances like mica and limestone, which serve critical roles in various industrial processes.

## Mica

Mica is a mineral characterized by a structure of series of plates or leaves that allow it to split easily into extremely thin sheets. These sheets can be layered so finely that a thousand layers can fit into a mica sheet only a few centimeters high. Mica occurs in various colors, including:

- Clear
- Black
- Green
- Red
- Yellow
- Brown

Because of its specific physical properties, mica is indispensable to the electric and electronic industries. Its primary industrial advantages include:

- Excellent di-electric strength
- Low power loss factor
- Strong insulating properties
- Resistance to high voltage

**Major Mica Producing Regions:**

- **Jharkhand:** The Koderma–Gaya–Hazaribagh belt located on the northern edge of the Chota Nagpur plateau is the leading producer.
- **Rajasthan:** Significant production occurs around the Ajmer area.
- **Andhra Pradesh:** The Nellore mica belt is another important producer in India.

## Limestone

Limestone is a sedimentary rock found in most geological formations. It is typically found in association with rocks composed of calcium carbonates or calcium and magnesium carbonates.

Limestone is vital for two primary industrial applications:

- **Cement Industry:** It serves as the fundamental raw material.
- **Iron Smelting:** It is an essential component used for smelting iron ore within blast furnaces.

**Key facts:**
- **Mica properties:** High di-electric strength, low power loss, and high voltage resistance.
- **Leading mica belt:** Koderma–Gaya–Hazaribagh (Jharkhand).
- **Limestone composition:** Calcium carbonates or calcium and magnesium carbonates.
- **Limestone uses:** Primary raw material for cement and essential for iron ore smelting.
