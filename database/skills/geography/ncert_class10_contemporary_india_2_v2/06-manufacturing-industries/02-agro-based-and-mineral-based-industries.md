This section examines industries categorized by their primary raw materials, focusing on agro-based sectors like sugar and mineral-based sectors including iron, steel, and aluminium.

## Agro-based Industries: Sugar

The sugar industry is a major agro-based sector in India. While India is the world's second-largest producer of sugar, it holds the first position in the production of *gur* and *khandsari*.

- **Characteristics:** The industry is seasonal in nature, making it ideally suited for the cooperative sector.
- **Raw Material:** Sugarcane is bulky, and its sucrose content reduces during haulage.
- **Regional Shifts:** While mills are widely distributed across states like Uttar Pradesh, Bihar, Maharashtra, Karnataka, Tamil Nadu, Andhra Pradesh, Gujarat, Punjab, Haryana, and Madhya Pradesh, there is a recent tendency to concentrate in southern and western states, particularly Maharashtra.
- **Reasons for Shift:** 
    - Cane produced in the south and west has a higher sucrose content.
    - A cooler climate ensures a longer crushing season.
    - Cooperatives have proven more successful in these regions.

## Mineral-based Industries

Mineral-based industries use minerals and metals as their primary raw materials.

### Iron and Steel Industry
Often regarded as the "basic industry," the iron and steel industry supports all other sectors—heavy, medium, and light—by providing necessary machinery. Steel is essential for engineering goods, construction, defense, medical equipment, and consumer goods.

- **Nature of Industry:** It is a heavy industry because both raw materials and finished goods are bulky, leading to high transportation costs.
- **Raw Material Ratio:** Production requires iron ore, coking coal, and limestone in a ratio of approximately 4:2:1. Manganese is also used to harden the steel.
- **Location:** The Chhotanagpur plateau region has the highest concentration of these industries due to low-cost iron ore, proximity to high-grade raw materials, cheap labour, and vast domestic market potential.

### Aluminium Smelting
Aluminium is the second most important metallurgical industry in India. It is a light, corrosion-resistant, and malleable metal that is a good conductor of heat.

- **Applications:** It is used for aircraft, utensils, and wires, and serves as a substitute for steel, copper, zinc, and lead.
- **Raw Material:** The industry uses bauxite, which is a bulky, dark reddish-coloured rock.
- **Location Factors:** Plants are located based on a regular supply of electricity and an assured, low-cost source of raw materials. Major locations include Odisha, West Bengal, Kerala, Uttar Pradesh, Chhattisgarh, Maharashtra, and Tamil Nadu.

**Key facts:**
- **Sugar production:** India is 2nd in sugar; 1st in *gur* and *khandsari*.
- **Steel raw material ratio:** Iron ore : Coking coal : Limestone = 4 : 2 : 1.
- **Aluminium raw material:** Bauxite.
- **Primary steel hub:** Chhotanagpur plateau.
